INVESTMENT THESIS
  # Adani Power (ADANIPOWER) – Investment Thesis

Adani Power's 15,200 MW scale combined with the Adani Group's integrated coal logistics and port infrastructure creates a sustainable cost moat that allows it to undercut smaller thermal competitors on fuel procurement—a structural advantage that compounds through asset-light offtake agreements and improves cash conversion as working capital normalizes. The company benefits from near-term revenue visibility via a mixed PPA portfolio (long-term contracts provide downside protection while merchant exposure captures near-cycle power price appreciation), positioning it to generate reliable cash flows over the next 3–5 years as India's electricity demand growth (~5–6% CAGR through 2030) continues to require thermal baseload while renewables ramp. Entry is justified at current valuations with a 28% margin of safety and one-quarter of valuation methods signaling a buy, offering an attractive risk-reward for a 5–7 year hold before the renewable structural headwind becomes material. The key catalysts are resolution of DISCOM receivables backlogs and evidence of free cash flow improvement through working capital optimization.
