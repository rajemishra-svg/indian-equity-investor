INVESTMENT THESIS
  # Coal India Limited (COALINDIA) – Investment Thesis

Coal India represents a rare opportunity to own a natural monopoly at a modest valuation discount: as a Maharatna PSU controlling 82% of India's domestic coal supply with an impenetrable regulatory moat, sovereign backing (63.13% GoI ownership), and captive demand from a thermal-dependent power sector, the company generates fortress-like returns (55.8% ROCE, 38% ROE, 90%+ cash conversion) with minimal execution risk. The structural tailwind is visibility: India's power demand will grow 6–8% annually through 2030, and even under aggressive renewable capacity expansion, thermal coal will remain the backbone of the grid (60%+ share), providing 5–7 years of volume and pricing stability before energy transition meaningfully pressures the core business. At a 7.8% margin of safety with valuation support from two methodologies in the buy zone, entry here offers an asymmetric risk/reward—downside protection from the monopoly's embedded cash generation and balance-sheet fortress, with upside optionality if India's decarbonization timeline slips or if management deploys capital shareholder-friendly (the company has begun special dividends). The thesis works for a 5–7 year horizon, not beyond.
